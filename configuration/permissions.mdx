M-TPM Portal requests only the permissions it genuinely needs to deliver its core functionality — no broad or unnecessary access is ever asked for. Rather than requesting everything at install time, the app follows Android best practice by asking for each permission **at the moment it is first needed**, so you always know exactly why a permission is being requested. This page describes each permission, what feature it supports, and how to manage it on your device.

---

## Permissions Overview

The table below summarises every permission M-TPM Portal may request, together with the features that depend on it.

| Permission | Required / Optional | Why It's Needed |
|---|---|---|
| **Camera** | Required (for scanning & AI features) | Scanning asset barcodes and QR codes; capturing images for OCR label auditing and visual inspection via the Gemini API. |
| **Storage / Media** | Required (for reports & attachments) | Saving exported maintenance reports to device storage; attaching photos from your gallery to maintenance records. |
| **Internet** | Required (for AI auditing) | Sending OCR audit requests to the Gemini API and communicating with any configured backend services. |
| **Notifications** | Optional | Displaying push notification reminders for scheduled and overdue maintenance tasks. |

### Camera

The Camera permission enables two critical workflows in M-TPM Portal:

- **Asset tag scanning** — Point your camera at a barcode or QR code on a physical asset to instantly look it up or log maintenance against it.
- **AI OCR auditing** — Capture a photo of an asset label; the image is sent to the Gemini API for automated inspection and data extraction.

### Storage / Media

The Storage permission (or granular media permissions on Android 13+) allows M-TPM Portal to:

- Write generated PDF or CSV maintenance reports to your device's **Downloads** folder.
- Read photos from your gallery when you choose to attach an existing image to a maintenance record rather than taking a new one.

### Internet

The Internet permission is a standard Android permission that is granted automatically (no runtime prompt). It is needed for:

- Calling the **Gemini API** for all AI-powered auditing operations.
- Connecting to any self-hosted backend service configured via `BASE_API_URL` in your `.env` file.

### Notifications

The Notifications permission is entirely optional. If you grant it, M-TPM Portal can send you:

- **Scheduled maintenance reminders** before a task is due.
- **Overdue task alerts** when a maintenance item has passed its deadline without completion.

You can adjust the types of notifications you receive at any time from the in-app Settings screen under **Notifications**.

## Granting Permissions

### Automatic Prompts (Recommended)

The simplest way to grant permissions is to let the app prompt you. The first time you use a feature that requires a permission, Android displays a system dialog asking you to allow or deny access. Grant the permission there and you're done — the app will remember your choice.

### Manual Grant via Device Settings

If you previously denied a permission and need to grant it later, follow these steps:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your device Settings">
    On your Android device, open the **Settings** app.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Apps">
    Tap **Apps** (sometimes labelled **Application Manager** or **App Management**, depending on your device manufacturer).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find M-TPM Portal">
    Scroll through the app list and tap **M-TPM Portal**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Permissions">
    Tap **Permissions** to view the full list of permissions the app has requested.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Toggle the permission on">
    Tap the permission you want to grant (e.g. **Camera**) and select **Allow** or **Allow only while using the app**, as appropriate for your security preference.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Revoking Permissions

You can revoke any permission at any time using the same **Settings → Apps → M-TPM Portal → Permissions** path described above. Tap a permission and select **Deny**. The table below shows the impact of revoking each permission.

| Revoked Permission | Impact |
|---|---|
| **Camera** | Asset tag scanning stops working. AI OCR auditing is disabled. You can still log maintenance manually by entering asset IDs by hand. |
| **Storage / Media** | Report export fails. You cannot attach gallery photos to records. Photos taken within the app are not saved to your device. |
| **Notifications** | Maintenance reminder and overdue task alerts stop. All in-app functionality remains unaffected. |

<Warning>
  Revoking the **Camera** permission disables both asset tag scanning **and** AI label auditing simultaneously, because both features share the same camera access. If your team relies on either workflow, ensure Camera permission remains granted on all devices running M-TPM Portal.
</Warning>

## Android 13+ Granular Media Permissions

<Note>
  On **Android 13 (API level 33) and above**, Google replaced the broad `READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE` permission with more granular alternatives. M-TPM Portal targets these new permissions appropriately:

  - **`READ_MEDIA_IMAGES`** — for reading photos from your gallery.
  - **`READ_MEDIA_VIDEO`** — only requested if video attachments are supported in your build.

  If your device runs Android 13 or later, you will see these specific permission prompts instead of a single "Storage" request. Granting `READ_MEDIA_IMAGES` is sufficient for all current M-TPM Portal photo attachment and export features.
</Note>
