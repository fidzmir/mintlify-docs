M-TPM Portal brings Total Productive Maintenance principles directly to your Android device, giving your team a structured, mobile-first system for capturing, monitoring, and reviewing maintenance activity across every asset on the production floor. Whether you are a technician recording a corrective repair, a supervisor checking live work-order status, or a plant manager auditing monthly maintenance trends, the app provides a single source of truth that travels with you — no paper forms, no desktop required.

## What is TPM in M-TPM Portal?

Total Productive Maintenance (TPM) is a company-wide strategy that aims to maximise equipment effectiveness by involving every employee — from operators to engineers — in proactive and preventive care of machinery. In the context of M-TPM Portal, TPM is operationalised through three tightly linked activities:

- **Logging** — capturing every maintenance event at the point of work, tied to a specific asset and technician.
- **Tracking** — monitoring the real-time status of open work orders so that nothing falls through the cracks.
- **Reviewing** — analysing historical records to identify recurring failures, measure response times, and support compliance audits.

Together these activities help your organisation shift from reactive "fix it when it breaks" maintenance toward a disciplined, data-driven approach that reduces unplanned downtime and extends asset life.

## Types of Maintenance Events

M-TPM Portal supports three core event types that align with standard TPM practices. When you create a new record, you will select one of these types to categorise the nature of the work:

| Event Type | When to Use |
|---|---|
| **Corrective** | The asset has already failed or is performing below specification and requires immediate repair to restore normal operation. |
| **Preventive** | Scheduled maintenance carried out at regular intervals (time-based or usage-based) to prevent failure before it occurs. |
| **Inspection** | A formal check or audit of an asset's condition, often linked to safety regulations or OEM service schedules, with no repair work performed. |

## How Maintenance Records Are Structured

Each maintenance record in M-TPM Portal is a self-contained document that captures everything relevant to a single maintenance event. The core fields are:

- **Asset** — the specific machine, line, or component the event relates to, identified by its registered asset ID or scanned tag.
- **Event Type** — one of Corrective, Preventive, or Inspection (see above).
- **Description** — a free-text summary of the work performed or the issue observed.
- **Start Time** — the date and time the event began, recorded at point of entry.
- **Estimated Completion** — the target date/time by which the work should be finished.
- **Status** — the current state of the work order: Pending, In Progress, Completed, or Overdue.
- **Technician** — the team member responsible for carrying out the work, drawn from your organisation's registered user list.
- **Notes** *(optional)* — additional context, safety observations, or follow-up actions.
- **Photo** *(optional)* — an attached image of the asset condition before or after work.

## Navigating to Maintenance

From the M-TPM Portal home dashboard, tap the **Maintenance** tile or select **Maintenance** from the bottom navigation bar. You will land on the Maintenance hub screen, which gives you quick access to all three phases:

- Tap **+ New Record** (top-right floating button) to jump straight to [Log Entry](/maintenance/log-entry).
- Tap **Active Orders** to open the [Tracking](/maintenance/tracking) dashboard.
- Tap **History** to browse and export [past records](/maintenance/history).

The app remembers your last-used filter settings, so returning to Tracking or History will restore your previous view automatically.
